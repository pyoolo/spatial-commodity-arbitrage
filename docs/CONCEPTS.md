# Key Concepts Cheat-Sheet

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **FOB** | Free On Board — price of the commodity loaded at the origin port. |
| **Delivered cost** | All-in per-tonne cost landed at destination: FOB + freight + handling + tariff + financing of cargo in transit. |
| **Arbitrage margin** | Destination price − delivered cost. Positive ⇒ "window open". |
| **No-arbitrage band** | Regional prices may differ only up to the transfer cost between them (spatial Law of One Price). |
| **Basis** | Local cash price − benchmark futures price. |
| **Cointegration** | Two non-stationary price series whose *spread* is stationary; the statistical signature of market integration. |
| **Half-life** | Time for a mean-reverting spread to close half the gap to its mean: ln(0.5)/ln(ρ). |
| **Spatial price equilibrium** | The competitive trade pattern; solves a min-cost transportation LP (Takayama–Judge). |
| **Shadow price (dual)** | Marginal value of one extra tonne of supply/demand at a location = equilibrium location price. |
| **Flat-price risk** | Exposure to the overall price level moving while a cargo is in transit; removed by selling futures. |
| **Crush spread** | (Time/form arbitrage, not modelled here) value of soybean products vs the seed. |

See `paper/geographic_arbitrage.pdf` for formal definitions and proofs.
